# 1ï¸âƒ£ **SDV Architecture**

## **A. Central Compute Architecture**

Modern SDVs are moving from dozens of independent ECUs â†’ to a small number of high-performance computers.

### **Layers**

1. **Hardware Layer**

   * High-performance SoC/CPU/GPU (NVIDIA, Qualcomm, Renesas, NXP)
   * Vehicle Ethernet backbone
   * Zonal controllers

2. **Middleware Layer**

   * AUTOSAR Adaptive
   * POSIX OS (Linux, QNX)
   * Hypervisors (separating safety & non-safety domains)
   * DDS or SOME/IP for service communication

3. **Application Layer**

   * Infotainment
   * ADAS / automated driving
   * Body control (HVAC, lighting)
   * Powertrain / battery management

---

## **B. Zonal Architecture**

Instead of ECUs everywhere, the car is divided into **zones**:

```
[ Front Left Zone ]   [ Front Right Zone ]
[ Rear Left Zone ]    [ Rear Right Zone ]
         |
    [ Central Compute ]
```

Zones handle I/O for all nearby sensors and actuators â†’ reducing wiring and cost.

---

## **C. High-Speed Networks**

* Automotive Ethernet (100BASE-T1 / 1000BASE-T1 / 10Gbit)
* CAN FD for legacy
* LIN for simple nodes
* FlexRay (declining)

Ethernet enables software-centric designs and wide data sharing.

# ğŸš— **SAE ADAS / Automation Levels (0â€“5)**

Standard: **SAE J3016**

# **LEVEL 0 â€” No Automation (Driver Controls Everything)**

Driver performs **all** steering, braking, and monitoring.
ADAS may assist momentarily:

* Forward collision warning
* Blind-spot alert
* Emergency braking
* Lane departure warning

**System does NOT control the car.**

---

# **LEVEL 1 â€” Driver Assistance**

The system can control **EITHER** steering **OR** acceleration/braking.

Examples:

* Adaptive Cruise Control (ACC)
* Lane Keeping Assist (LKA)

**Driver must monitor environment and keep hands on wheel.**

---

# **LEVEL 2 â€” Partial Automation**

The system can control **both** steering **AND** acceleration/braking simultaneously.
Still **driver-supervised**.

Examples:

* Tesla Autopilot
* GM Super Cruise
* Mercedes Distronic + LKA
* Hyundai Highway Driving Assist (HDA)

**Driver must stay engaged and ready to take over.**

---

# **LEVEL 3 â€” Conditional Automation**

System drives **entirely** under specific conditions.
Driver does **not** need to supervise, but must be ready to take over when requested.

Examples:

* Mercedes-Benz Drive Pilot (approved in some regions)
* Honda Sensing Elite (Japan)

Conditions: traffic jam, mapped highways, etc.
**System monitors environment, not the driver.**

---

# **LEVEL 4 â€” High Automation**

System drives itself **without human intervention** in specific geofenced areas.

Examples:

* Waymo Robotaxi
* Cruise Robotaxi (paused in some regions)

If system fails â†’ **it handles fallback**, NOT the human.

The car cannot operate everywhere.

---

# **LEVEL 5 â€” Full Automation**

System drives the car **everywhere** under **all conditions**.
No steering wheel needed.
